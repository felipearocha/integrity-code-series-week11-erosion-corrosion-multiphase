1. Multiphase Flow — Beggs-Brill (1973)

1.1 No-slip holdup and Froude number

$$ \lambda_L \;=\; \frac{q_L}{q_L + q_g}, \qquad \mathrm{Fr} \;=\; \frac{u_m^{2}}{g\,D}, \qquad u_m \;=\; \frac{q_L + q_g}{A} $$

SOURCE: Beggs & Brill (1973) JPT 607–617, SPE-4007-PA.

Code: src/multiphase_flow.py → resolve_flow, beggs_brill_regime.

1.2 Horizontal liquid holdup by regime

$$ E_L(0) \;=\; \frac{a\,\lambda_L^{\,b}}{\mathrm{Fr}^{\,c}}, \qquad E_L(0) \ge \lambda_L $$ with $(a,b,c)$ selected per flow regime (segregated / intermittent / distributed) from the regime boundaries $L_1\!-\!L_4$.

SOURCE: Beggs & Brill (1973), Table of regime coefficients.

Code: src/multiphase_flow.py → beggs_brill_holdup.

1.3 Transition regime — A-weighted blend

$$ E_L \;=\; A\,E_L^{\text{seg}} \;+\; (1-A)\,E_L^{\text{int}}, \qquad A \;=\; \frac{L_3 - \mathrm{Fr}}{L_3 - L_2} $$
The transition holdup is the Froude-weighted interpolation between the segregated and intermittent correlations — not a flat 0.5 average (audit correction).

Code: src/multiphase_flow.py → beggs_brill_holdup (regime="transition").

2. Hydrodynamics — Wall Shear & Mass Transfer

2.1 NORSOK wall shear stress (Eq.21-22)

$$ \tau_w \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\,\rho_m\,f\,u_m^{2}, \qquad f \;=\; 0.001375\left[\,1 + \left(\frac{2\times10^{4}\,\varepsilon}{D} + \frac{10^{6}\,\mu_m}{\rho_m\,u_m\,D}\right)^{\!1/3}\,\right] $$
The friction bracket is raised to the cube root $1/3$ (audit correction), not $0.33$.

SOURCE: NORSOK M-506:2017 Eqs.21–22.

Code: src/hydrodynamics.py → norsok_wall_shear.

2.2 Berger-Hau mass transfer

$$ \mathrm{Sh} \;=\; 0.0165\,\mathrm{Re}^{0.86}\,\mathrm{Sc}^{0.33}, \qquad k_m \;=\; \frac{\mathrm{Sh}\,D_{AB}}{D} $$
The mass-transfer coefficient and the Beggs-Brill holdup are computed as diagnostics; the flow effect on corrosion is carried by the NORSOK wall-shear term $\tau_w$ (as NORSOK M-506 intends), not by $k_m$ or $E_L$.

SOURCE: Berger & Hau (1977) Int. J. Heat Mass Transfer 20:1185–1194.

Code: src/hydrodynamics.py → sherwood_berger_hau, mass_transfer_coefficient.

2.3 Elbow angular shear profile — prescribed shape [T3]

The angular distribution of wall shear around the 90° elbow is a prescribed analytical shape (two hand-fitted Gaussian bumps) whose peak locations are set to match the pattern reported by El-Gammal (2010) / Kim (2021) — an extrados feature near 37° and a stronger intrados inlet peak. It is an input shape fitted to the experiment, not a resolved CFD field.

SOURCE: peak locations — El-Gammal et al. (2010) Nucl. Eng. Des. 240:1589; Kim et al. (2021) Nucl. Eng. Tech. 53:3003. Profile shape = tuned fit [T3].

Code: src/hydrodynamics.py → elbow_angular_shear_profile.

3. Sand Erosion — DNV-RP-O501 Rev.4.2

3.1 Characteristic bend impact angle

$$ \alpha \;=\; \arctan\!\left(\frac{1}{2\sqrt{R/D}}\right) $$

SOURCE: DNV-RP-O501 Rev.4.2 (2007), Sec.8.4.

Code: src/erosion_dnv.py → characteristic_bend_angle.

3.2 Bend erosion rate (Eq.8.15-8.21)

$$ \dot{E}_L \;=\; \left[\frac{K\,\dot{m}_p\,U_p^{\,n}\,F(\alpha)}{\rho_t\,A_t}\right] C_1\,C_{\text{unit}}, \qquad A_t \;=\; \frac{\pi D^{2}}{4\sin\alpha} $$
The angle dependence is carried by $F(\alpha)$ and by the $1/\sin\alpha$ inside $A_t$. There is no additional standalone $\sin\alpha$ in the numerator — adding one double-counts the impact angle and underpredicts bend erosion by a factor $\sin\alpha$ (audit fix, ~2.6× at $R/D=1.5$).

SOURCE: DNV-RP-O501 Rev.4.2 Eqs.8.15–8.21; carbon steel $K=2.0\times10^{-9}$, $n=2.6$, $C_1=2.5$ (Table 7-2); $F(\alpha)$ 8-term polynomial (Table 7-1).

Code: src/erosion_dnv.py → erosion_bend, f_alpha.

4. CO₂ Corrosion — NORSOK M-506:2017 (Rev.3)

4.1 Corrosion rate (Eq.1)

$$ \mathrm{CR}_t \;=\; K_t\;f_{\mathrm{CO_2}}^{\,0.62}\;\left(\frac{S}{19}\right)^{0.146 + 0.0324\,\log_{10} f_{\mathrm{CO_2}}}\;f(\mathrm{pH})_t $$ with the 9-node $K_t$ temperature table (5–150 °C; separate low-T forms at 5 °C and 15 °C), $S=\tau_w$ the wall shear, and $f(\mathrm{pH})_t$ from Table 2.

SOURCE: NORSOK M-506:2017 Eq.1, Tables 1–2.

Code: src/corrosion_norsok.py → corrosion_rate, f_ph.

4.2 CO₂ fugacity (Eqs.4, 7-8)

$$ f_{\mathrm{CO_2}} \;=\; a\,p_{\mathrm{CO_2}}, \qquad a \;=\; 10^{\,P\left(0.0031 - \frac{1.4}{T}\right)}\;\;(P<250\ \text{bar}) $$
$f_{\mathrm{CO_2}}$ is clamped to the NORSOK applicability window $[0.1,\,10]$ bar so an out-of-range fugacity is not silently extrapolated through the power law (audit guard).

SOURCE: NORSOK M-506:2017 Eq.4, Clause 8.1 Eqs.7–8.

5. Erosion-Corrosion Synergy — ASTM G119

5.1 Additive decomposition

$$ T \;=\; W_0 + C_0 + S, \qquad S \;=\; \Delta C_w + \Delta W_c $$ where $W_0$ is pure erosion, $C_0$ is scale-protected corrosion, $\Delta C_w$ is erosion-enhanced corrosion, and $\Delta W_c$ is corrosion-enhanced erosion.

SOURCE: ASTM G119 Standard Guide for Determining Synergism Between Wear and Corrosion.

Code: src/synergy_g119.py → decompose.

5.2 Scale-removal model [HEURISTIC / T3]

$$ f_{\text{rm}} \;=\; 1 - (1-f_\tau)(1-f_E), \quad f_\tau = 1 - e^{-\tau_w/\tau_c}, \quad f_E = 1 - e^{-W_0/E_c} $$ $$ \Delta C_w \;=\; f_{\text{rm}}\,(C_{\text{bare}} - C_0), \qquad \Delta W_c \;=\; 0.25\,W_0\,f_{\text{rm}} $$
The threshold $\tau_c = 15$ Pa, the erosion scale $E_c = 0.5$ mm/yr, and the $0.25$ coefficient are heuristic (T3) parameters. ASTM G119 supplies the additive framework, not the predictive split; NORSOK M-506 has no quantitative descaling law. The bare-steel rate $C_{\text{bare}}$ is a T3 proxy (NORSOK evaluated at elevated shear).

Code: src/synergy_g119.py → scale_removal_fraction, decompose.

6. Wall-Loss Field & Time-to-Perforation

Cumulative metal loss over the elbow surface is bounded by the wall thickness — the pipe perforates first and cannot thin further: $$ \text{loss}(\theta,\varphi,t) \;=\; \min\!\bigl(\dot{L}(\theta,\varphi)\cdot t,\; t_{\text{wall}}\bigr), \qquad t_{\text{perf}} \;=\; \frac{t_{\text{wall}}}{\dot{L}_{\text{peak}}} $$ with $t_{\text{wall}} = 12.7$ mm.

SOURCE: metal loss is physically bounded by the wall (practitioner constraint) [T3].

Code: src/elbow_field.py → wall_loss_field, time_to_perforation.

7. Fitness-for-Service — API 579-1 Part 5

Level-1 Remaining Strength Factor for a local thin area, with the Folias bulging factor $M_t$: $$ R_{t,\text{loss}} \;=\; \frac{t_{\text{nom}} - t_{\text{act}}}{t_{\text{nom}}}, \qquad M_t \;=\; \sqrt{1 + 0.48\,\lambda^{2}}, \qquad \lambda \;=\; \frac{1.285\,L}{\sqrt{R\,t}} $$ $$ \mathrm{RSF} \;=\; \frac{1 - R_{t,\text{loss}}}{1 - R_{t,\text{loss}}/M_t}, \qquad \mathrm{RSF}_a = 0.90 $$ A longer flaw (larger $M_t$) gives a lower RSF at the same depth.

SOURCE: API 579-1/ASME FFS-1 Part 5, Level 1 RSF; Folias bulging factor.
